# Summary: A Case for Defensive Mining

Published April 29, 2026. Argues that commercial entities with genuine business interests on a blockchain have rational incentive to mine defensively — not purely for profit, but to insure their on-chain operations.

---

## Greed and Vulnerability

Standard PoW security assumes profit-seeking miners. Vulnerabilities this creates:

- Majority hash holders can execute various attacks
- Profit-driven actors may find attacking a chain more lucrative than securing it
- Intermittent censorship damages time-sensitive services
- Anonymous mining concentration is a real risk — the article cites evidence that **"a single custodian seems to control 47% of the total Bitcoin mining power"**
- Miners may abandon chains during price downturns, creating vulnerability windows

Despite these risks, PoW remains the superior consensus mechanism for permissionless blockchains.

## Mining Pools

Mining pools reduce participation barriers through distributed rewards but operate primarily from self-interest, often in lightly regulated jurisdictions where external influence remains possible.

## The Gajumaru Approach

Groot serves as a decentralized PoW minting authority that "safely connects Associate Chains, which can be properly regulated, private or otherwise." This architecture enables GAJU to function as actual money while supporting regulated digital currencies within an interconnected ecosystem.

## The Defensive Miner

The key conceptual shift: when commercial participants have on-chain business operations, contributing mining power becomes a rational choice **beyond direct profitability**. Mining becomes insurance protecting core business interests.

## Regulated Mining Pools

Mining pools operating within regulated jurisdictions can:
- Publicly commit to content-neutral block production policies
- Make censorship breaches subject to contract litigation
- Allow commercial users to join pools within their own jurisdictions

This combines network security with legal recourse for censorship violations.
