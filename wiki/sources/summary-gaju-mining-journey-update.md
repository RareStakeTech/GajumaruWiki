## Ulf's Role — Hive Mining

Ulf built the hive mining infrastructure from scratch:

> "Ulf has had lots of fun with the task of: hey, we're going to do this hive mining concept. Hey, just poop out a whole hive mining concept and make everything work with dividend-writing contracts that tie it all together on the blockchain."

This is the SaaS mining model — Hive miners managed by QPQ, with smart contracts automatically distributing Gaju rewards to license holders. A global first, done without any prior art to reference.

---

## Primary Audience: Small Business Owners

> "The crypto bros aren't really our audience. It's small business owners are our primary adopters and they're not crypto experts. They're experts at all kinds of other things but not crypto."

Security vs usability trade-off is central. The software must be usable by people with no crypto background while remaining genuinely secure. The 300+ pre-release miners were the test bed for this.

---

## The Payment Provider Rugpull

A payment provider abandoned QPQ at launch, cascading into broader platform failures. This forced the team to build their own web shop from scratch with zero external dependencies — on top of all other work.

> "We got rugpulled by payment providers that wound up having a cascading impact on our technology. Our platforms that we'd chosen suddenly became inadequate. The task becomes: also just poop out a web shop with no dependencies by last week."

The Year in Review notes "first payment friction resolved December 2025" — this is the resolution of this crisis.

---

## Payment Censorship as Validation

The speaker uses high-profile debanking examples to validate QPQ's mission:
- **Michael Nolles**: debanked by Stripe; can't receive monetization from X platform; cause unclear
- **Steam/gaming**: Mastercard/Visa can now effectively ban games from Steam via T&C; legality irrelevant

Craig's framing (referenced): *"It's not about making things illegal, it's about making them impossible."*

## Over 300 Pre-Release Miners

> "Over 300 of them came in and bought into this pre-release and substantially, and they were the guinea pigs for all of the developments. Their feedback was extraordinarily valuable because it meant that we learned things now and we made changes now."

Consistent with Year in Review figure of 300+ active miners. This community sustained the project through the hardest period.

Adam Percy is a house builder who joined on day one. He exemplifies the community QPQ attracted: non-crypto people motivated by the vision, who "proactively roll up their sleeves" to help newcomers. He became the community's best advocate by simplifying onboarding for everyone else.
