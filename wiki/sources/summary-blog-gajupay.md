# Summary: GajuPay

Published March 26, 2026. QPQ AG's commercial pitch for GajuPay as a blockchain-enabled point-of-sale payment solution targeting the broken payment processing industry.

---

## The Market Opportunity

Global payment processing market:
- 2022: **$47.61 billion**
- 2030 projection: **$139.90 billion**
- CAGR: **14.5%**

Despite explosive growth, merchants often operate at **minimal or negative profit margins** due to processor and interchange economics.

## The Broken Economics — Japanese Card Example

On a ¥10,000 transaction at 1.99% processing fee:
- Gross: **¥190**
- Domestic ops cost: **−¥10**
- Interchange fees: **−¥180**
- International brand fee: **−¥80**
- **Net: −¥70 to −¥80 loss per transaction**

The processor loses money on every transaction. The merchant pays the fees. Neither is well-served.

## GajuPay's Solution

Blockchain-enabled P2P payment processing for merchants via GajuMobile:
- Direct peer-to-peer transactions
- Reduced or eliminated intermediary fees
- Fiat-to-Gaju conversion rate logging for tax compliance
- Multi-register support (multiple registers → one merchant account)
- Refund processing with proper authorization
- Anti-fraud controls

## Implementation Roadmap

**Stage 1 — Web Portal (MVP):** Browser-based solution with basic sales tracking and accounting exports. Minimal setup costs, accessible to small merchants.

**Stage 2 — Webshop Integration:** Plugin architecture and widgets for e-commerce platform integration.

**Stage 3 — Self-Hosted Backend:** Enterprise option for data sovereignty and regulatory compliance.

## Launch Status

Scheduled for **2026 alongside GajuMobile**. Referral partnerships via **partners@qpq.swiss**.
