# DLTs (QPQ Public Wiki)

QPQ's foundational definition of Distributed Ledger Technologies, authored by [[peter]] (Peter Harpending).

A **public ledger** is readable by anyone. A **distributed** public ledger allows anyone to hold a complete copy (or partial copy as needed). Examples: LDAP, replicating databases, DNS, blockchains.

The central challenge in all DLTs is the consensus mechanism — the agreed process for deciding what gets recorded and who may update the ledger.
