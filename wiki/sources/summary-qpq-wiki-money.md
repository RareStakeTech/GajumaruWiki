# Money / The Nature of Money (QPQ Public Wiki)

QPQ's foundational analysis of money and currency, authored by [[peter]] (Peter Harpending).

## Three Functions of Money

1. **Medium of exchange** — facilitates trade between dissimilar goods
2. **Store of value** — preserves purchasing power over time
3. **Means of settlement** — a way to value and pay debts

## Money as Emergent Property

> "Money is an emergent property of market systems."

Money arises spontaneously when dissimilar goods need to be exchanged. It is not invented; it emerges. This distinguishes money (the category) from currency (a specific implementation of it).

## What a Functional Currency Requires

- **Liquidity** — easily convertible to other things
- **Easy divisibility** — can be split into useful denominations
- **Fungibility** — each unit is interchangeable with any other unit
- **Non-expert accessibility** — ordinary people can use it without special knowledge

## Value Foundation

Currency value rests on:
> "A belief shared among market participants in the future value of the currency itself."

While subjective, robust currencies anchor this belief in measurable characteristics and enforcement mechanisms (e.g., legal tender status, network effects, technical properties).

Critically: **"Any objective threat to a currency's future value has an immediate impact on prices denominated in that currency, regardless of the current money supply."** Market perception precedes and overrides monetary quantity in price determination.
