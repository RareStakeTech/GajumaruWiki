# LTIN — Liechtenstein Trust Integrity Network

LTIN is a sovereign blockchain infrastructure for Liechtenstein's national digital economy, built on Gajumaru's [[associate-chains]] architecture.

## Announced
October 22, 2025 — six days after first Gaju token circulation.

## Ownership Structure
- **Majority owner**: Telecom Liechtenstein
- **Founding technical partner & shareholder**: [[qpq-ag]]
- **Founding participants**: QPQ AG, Bank Frick, Bitcoin Suisse, Swiss Subnet, Zilliqa

## Regulatory Framework
- Operates under Liechtenstein Blockchain Act
- EU MiCAR compliant from launch
- Liechtenstein is an EEA member, giving EU market access

## Launch Timeline
Q2/Q3/Q4 2026 (phased)

## Strategic Significance
- First sovereign [[associate-chains]] deployment on Gajumaru
- Validates Gajumaru architecture for national-level implementation
- Template replicable for other nations
- "Proof point" for government partnership inquiries
