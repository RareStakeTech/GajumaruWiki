## On the Nature of Money

Money has three primary functions:

1. **Medium of exchange**
2. **Store of value**
3. **Means of settlement** (valuing and paying debts)

"Money is an emergent property of market systems" that arises when dissimilar goods need to be exchanged. The distinction is made between money as a broad category and currency as a specific implementation.

### Currency Requirements

A functional currency must possess these characteristics:
- Liquidity
- Easy divisibility
- Fungibility
- Accessibility for non-experts

### Value Foundation

Currency value stems from "a belief shared among market participants in the future value of the currency itself." While subjective, robust currencies ground this belief in measurable characteristics and enforcement mechanisms.

Notably: "any objective threat to a currency's future value has an immediate impact on prices denominated in that currency, _regardless of the current money supply_." This underscores how market perception influences pricing independently of monetary quantity.
