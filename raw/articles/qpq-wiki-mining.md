## Mining

Following from the Proof of Work concept, "mining" involves multiple blockchain nodes competing for recognition as the temporary leader. On Gajumaru, miners solve "cuckoo cycle" puzzles—finding cycles within a deterministically generated node map from a seed number.

Unlike most blockchains that use numerical hashing, the cuckoo cycle algorithm prioritizes memory access over CPU capacity, making it "the least computation-heavy, and therefore most energy-efficient, of the known PoW algorithms."

The process can be visualized as people at desks completing jigsaw puzzles; whoever finishes first becomes the new leader and receives the master ledger copy, gaining authority to include pending updates from the mempool.

Miners earn rewards in two forms: new coins for winning the puzzle competition, and additional compensation for including transactions. This dual-reward system drives coin supply growth over time as miners maintain the competitive ledger leadership process.
