## Distributed Ledger Technologies (DLTs)

A public ledger is one that anyone can read. A distributed public ledger allows anyone to possess a complete copy, or perhaps only the portions they need. Examples include LDAP, replicating databases, DNS, and blockchains.

The central challenge in such systems involves reaching agreement among all users about what should be recorded and who may update it. The mechanism selected for achieving this common agreement is termed the "consensus mechanism" of the ledger.
